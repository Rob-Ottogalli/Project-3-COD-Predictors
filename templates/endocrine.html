Endocrine, Nutritional, and Metabolical Diseases

Prominent Diseases:

  • Diabetes mellitus (70.1% of deaths)
  • Metabolic disorders (18.9% of deaths)
  • Obesity and other hyperalimentation (5.6% of deaths)
Endocrine glands produce hormones that help regulate and control bodily processes. Endocrine diseases occur when an endocrine gland produces too much or too little of its respective hormone(s). The most common endocrine disease is diabetes, accounting for 70% of deaths in this category. Diabetes occurs when the body is unable to produce an adequate amount of insulin, causing blood sugar levels to be abnormally high. The second category, metabolic disorders, is a general category that encompasses a wide variety of metabolic disorders primarily focused on amino-acid metabolism and glycolysis.
