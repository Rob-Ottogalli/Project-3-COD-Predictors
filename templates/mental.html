Dementia and other amnestic disorders account for the vast majority of deaths in this category. This sub-category is most heavily influenced by age, with 99.9% of deaths in the dementia subcategory being individuals over the age of 55 and 68% being over the age of 85. Because individuals aged over 85 years represent a majority of the deaths, the data is skewed towards females (64.4% of deaths) because female life expectancy (81.1) is higher than male life expectancy (76.1).
