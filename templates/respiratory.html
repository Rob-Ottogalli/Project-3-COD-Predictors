Diseases of the respiratory system

Prominent Diseases:

  • Chronic lower respiratory diseases (57.5% of deaths)
    • Asthma
    • Bronchitis
    • Emphysema
  • Influenza and pneumonia (22.0% of deaths)
Chronic lower respiratory diseases account for over 50% of deaths related to the respiratory system. These diseases are heavily influenced by prolonged exposure to inhaled irritants such as cigarette smoke, air pollution, dust, or toxic gases. Viruses are also well-known causes of respiratory disease. Viruses typically cause respiratory disease because these pathogens are most commonly ingested by inhalation. Common symptoms related to respiratory disease are coughing, shortness of breath, and chest pain.
