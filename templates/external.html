External causes of morbidity and mortality

Top Causes of Death:

  • Other external causess (46.2% of deaths)
    • Falling/Drowning
    • Exposure to electric current, radiation, extreme temperature
    • Exposure to smoke, fire, flames
  • Transport Accidents (20.5% of deaths)
    • Car/Motorcycle/Bus Accidents
    • Pedestrian/Bicycle Accidents
    • Other land/water/air transport
  • Intentional Self-Harm (20.0% of deaths)
  • Assault (8.6% of deaths)
External causes of morbidity is a unique category in that age has an inverse effect on risk compared to other categories. Sub-chapters in this category that are particularly skewed towards younger individuals are transport accidents, intentional self-harm, and assault. This category is also heavily skewed towards males, with males accounting for 71.2% of the total deaths.
